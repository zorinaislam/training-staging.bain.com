If you’re encountering an “Exchange password required” message on your Mac, it typically means that your Mac is having trouble connecting to your Exchange email account, and it needs you to re-enter your password. Here are steps to resolve this issue:<\/p>\n

    \n
  1. Open the “Mail” app on your Mac.<\/li>\n
  2. In the top menu bar, click on “Mail” and select “Accounts.”<\/li>\n
  3. In the left sidebar, select the Exchange account that is showing the password required message.<\/li>\n
  4. You will likely see a message indicating that your account needs attention or that the password is required. Click on this message.<\/li>\n
  5. A window will appear, asking you to enter your password. Make sure you enter your Exchange email account password correctly.<\/li>\n
  6. After entering the password, click “Sign In” or “Next.”<\/li>\n
  7. Your Mac will attempt to verify your password and connect to the Exchange server. If the password is correct and there are no other issues, your account should be re-authenticated, and the “Exchange password required” message should disappear.<\/li>\n
  8. If the problem persists or if you’re still getting the message after entering the correct password, you may want to check your internet connection and ensure that the Exchange server settings in your Mail account are accurate.<\/li>\n<\/ol>\n

    If you continue to experience issues, you may want to contact your IT department or email service provider for further assistance, as there could be issues with your Exchange server configuration or account settings.<\/p>\n
